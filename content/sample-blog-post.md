## The Technology Behind AI Tile Image Search

### Computer Vision: Teaching Machines to "See"

Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence that trains computers to interpret and understand visual information. For tile image search, the AI must recognize:

**Visual Features:**
- **Patterns**: Geometric shapes, organic designs, repeating motifs
- **Colors**: Primary colors, secondary hues, color palettes
- **Textures**: Glossy vs. matte, smooth vs. rough, 3D embossing
- **Styles**: Modern, traditional, rustic, industrial
- **Materials**: Ceramic, porcelain, natural stone, glass

### The Image Analysis Process

Here's what happens when you upload a tile photo to a visual search engine like AxoVision:

#### **Step 1: Image Preprocessing**
The AI first prepares the image:
- Resizes to optimal dimensions
- Adjusts lighting and contrast
- Removes noise and artifacts
- Enhances edges and features
- Corrects perspective distortion

#### **Step 2: Feature Extraction**
The AI identifies key visual features using deep learning neural networks:
- **Color histograms**: Distribution of colors across the image
- **Edge detection**: Identifies boundaries and shapes
- **Pattern recognition**: Locates repeating elements
- **Texture analysis**: Evaluates surface characteristics
- **Spatial relationships**: How elements are arranged

#### **Step 3: Vector Embedding**
The AI converts the image into a mathematical representation called a "feature vector"—a list of numbers that captures the essence of the image. Similar-looking tiles will have similar vectors.

#### **Step 4: Similarity Search**
The AI compares the query image's vector against millions of tile images in the database:
- Calculates **cosine similarity** or **Euclidean distance**
- Ranks results by similarity score
- Applies filters (price, availability, material)
- Returns top matches in milliseconds

#### **Step 5: Ranking and Refinement**
The system ranks results based on:
- Visual similarity score
- User preferences and history
- Business rules (featured products, stock availability)
- Contextual factors (room type, style preferences)

### Pattern Recognition Technology

Pattern recognition is crucial for tile matching. The AI must distinguish between:

**Geometric Patterns:**
- Hexagons, chevrons, herringbone
- Moroccan, arabesque, quatrefoil
- Grid patterns, basketweave, pinwheel

**Organic Patterns:**
- Floral, botanical, nature-inspired
- Marble veining, wood grain
- Abstract, watercolor effects

**Complex Patterns:**
- Multi-element designs
- Layered patterns
- Gradient transitions

Modern AI uses **Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)**—the same technology that powers facial recognition—to identify these patterns with 95%+ accuracy.

### Color Matching Algorithms

Color is often the first thing people notice in a tile. The AI analyzes:

**Color Extraction:**
- Dominant colors (the main 3-5 colors)
- Color proportions (how much of each color)
- Color relationships (complementary, analogous)
- Undertones (warm vs. cool)

**Color Space Analysis:**
The AI works in multiple color spaces:
- **RGB**: Red, Green, Blue (how screens display color)
- **HSV**: Hue, Saturation, Value (more perceptually uniform)
- **LAB**: Lightness, A (green-red), B (blue-yellow) (best for matching)

This ensures that if you upload a photo of a "sage green" tile, you'll get results for sage green—not forest green or mint green.

### Texture Analysis

Texture adds depth and tactile quality to tiles:

**Texture Features:**
- **Surface finish**: Glossy, matte, textured, polished
- **Relief depth**: Flat vs. 3D embossed
- **Pattern scale**: Fine detail vs. bold design
- **Material characteristics**: Natural stone, ceramic glaze, metallic

The AI uses **Gabor filters** and **Local Binary Patterns (LBP)** to quantify these texture characteristics.
