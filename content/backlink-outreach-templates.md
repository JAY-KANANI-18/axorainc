## Outreach Best Practices

### âœ… Do's:
- **Personalize every email** - mention specific content, use their name
- **Lead with value** - what's in it for them?
- **Keep it concise** - respect their time (250-400 words max)
- **Follow up once** - after 5-7 days if no response
- **Build relationships** - engage with their content first
- **Be genuine** - no spammy or overly salesy language
- **Proofread** - typos destroy credibility

### âŒ Don'ts:
- Don't use generic "Dear Webmaster" templates
- Don't ask for links in your first email
- Don't send to irrelevant sites
- Don't be pushy or aggressive
- Don't send the same email to multiple people at one site
- Don't use fake flattery
- Don't promise links you won't deliver

## Email Subject Line Tips

**High-Performing Subject Lines:**
- Question format: "Quick question about [Their Content]"
- Specific value: "Found a broken link on [Their Site]"
- Collaboration: "Collaboration idea for [Their Audience]"
- Personal: "Loved your article on [Topic]"
- Direct: "Guest post suggestion: [Specific Title]"

**Low-Performing Subject Lines:**
- "Link exchange"
- "Business opportunity"
- "I found your site"
- "Partnership inquiry"
- Anything too long (>60 characters)

---

## Response Rate Expectations

**Industry Averages:**
- **Initial response rate**: 10-15%
- **Positive responses**: 5-8%
- **Actual links acquired**: 2-5%

**Tips to Improve Response Rates:**
1. Send on Tuesday-Thursday (best days)
2. Send 10-11 AM in their timezone
3. Personalize beyond the first name
4. Follow up once after 5-7 days
5. Engage with their content before reaching out

## Quick Personalization Research

**Before sending, spend 5 minutes:**
1. Read their latest 2-3 articles
2. Check their Twitter for recent tweets
3. Look at their LinkedIn profile
4. Note any recent company news
5. Find genuine common ground

**Personalization Examples:**
- "Congrats on [Recent Achievement]"
- "I saw you recently [Something They Did]"
- "Your take on [Topic] resonated with me because..."
